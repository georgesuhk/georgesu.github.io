Density Functional Theory (DFT) Based High Throughput Screening

On going

A project under a Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS) with the Imperial Solid State Physics department. The aim is to create a High Throughput Screening infrastructure to facilitate the investigation the effects of molecular structure on the reorganization energy of organic semi-conductors. In the project, the infrastructure was used to specifically investigate the reorganization energy associated with PET (Photoinduced Charge Transfer) events. The specific result are private to the group, but the infrastructure design process and features are shared here.

Background

Reorganization energy or λis the energy associated with the change in a molecules geometry following a transfer of charge. Such as illustrated in the following charge transfer event between a donor molecule (losing electron) and the acceptor molecule (gaining electron):


The study of this energy is important as the rate of charge transfer, and hence the rate electrical energy can be harnessed from Photoinduced Electron Transfer (PET) events (relevant in Organic Photovoltaic Cells and photosynthesis) is highly dependent on reorganization energy. Generally, we want to make materials with lower reorganization energy.

Computational infrastructure

Vision

The aim to is create a large scale, systematic and intelligent infrastructure for exploring chemical structure space. We wish to have the ability to efficiency screen large batches of molecules for their reorganization energy and other insightful chemical properties. The data is then interpreted and used to guide the design of new molecular design, which are fed back into screening and the cycle repeats. This format of screening allows us to obtained optimized chemical structures, and allows for the implementation of machine learning for the design of chemical structures.


Computational machinery

The main DFT software leveraged in the project is Gaussian. However significant post-processing is required to obtain the desire information for the project. An adapted version of the Dushin package is used which is able to calculate the reorganization energy split amongst the normal modes of the particle. This allows for us to then identify the common sources of high reorganization energy. Another crucial post processing software is TheoDORE (Theoretical Density, Orbital Relaxation, and Exciton analysis), which has the ability to perform fragment based analysis of the excited state of a molecule, which helps us classify how separated the charges are in a particular excited state of a molecule.



Highlighted features

Here is how the structure works overall and some of its features

  • Input: a list of “SMILES” strings. (A format of representing chemical molecules as strings based on their structure).

  • Generating molecules from SMILES strings, containing 3D positions of each atom.

  • Local optimizations using a forcefield using OpenBabel, ensuring the coordinates of the molecule is valid before feeding into DFT

  • Identifying the donor and acceptor substructures within the molecule using SMARTS (SMILES arbitrary target specification). This step is necessary for the fragment based excited state analysis of TheoDORE

  • Automated generation of bash scripts, allocated appropriate memory and time. (Dependent on the size of the molecule and nature of calculation.)

  • Automated upload to HPC service

  • Automatically detecting when the job is completed, and running Dushin and TheoDORE as necessary.

    • Dushin output for Benzene, Naphthalene and Anthracene


  • Csv files containing the relevant information is transferred to a local folder to be cleaned and entered into a systematic database.

Database

The next part to develop a tool for systematic study is to make a database with the following requirements:

  • Self-generate using local files (Updated easily when files are updated)
  • Easily accessed and indexed (Cable to quickly return subsections relevant to eg. a specific type of molecules)
  • Facilitates easy comparison between molecular designs
  • Populated with supplementary chemical property information for supervised

Due to these considerations, I decided to use a multi index Pandas to construct the data frame as it can be easily generated from files, is very lightweight, supports conditional indexing and has inbuilt plotting capabilities as well as support for packages such as seaborn for more advanced visualisation.
