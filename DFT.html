Density Functional Theory (DFT) Simulations

On going

A project under a Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS) with the Imperial Physics department. The aim is to use DFT simulations to investigate the effects of molecular structure on the reorganization energy of organic semi-conductors. The reorganization energy is the energy cost of changing geometry when molecule experience excitation. A lower reorganization energy will increase the efficiency of charge transfer, which is highly desirable in OLED and OPV technologies.

Source classification, and normal modes

The main DFT software leveraged in the project is Gaussian. However significant post-processing is required to obtain the desire information for the project. An adapted version of the Dushin package is used which is able to calculate the reorganization energy split amongst the normal modes of the particle. This allows for us to then identify the common sources of high reorganization energy.


As shown above, these are split into contributions from low frequency vibrations: Torsion of the molecule, mid frequency vibrations: C=C stretching and breathing and high-frequency vibrations: C-H bond vibrations.

Automated script and supercomputers

One of the main components missing of the group's previous reseach into the area is the lack of a systematic study facilitated by an automated workflow and a rigorous yet accessible database. The first program I developed is a script capable of automating the calculation of reorganization energy spread amongst normal modes, and other important properties of the desired molecule of study. The process is as follows:

  • Input: a list of “SMILES” strings. (A format of representing chemical molecules as strings based on their structure).

  • Generating molecules from SMILES strings, containing 3D positions of each atom.

  • Submitting the molecule onto Imperial’s supercomputer to undergo DFT calculations.

  • Automatically senses when the job has been finished, and stores calculated variables and contextual information regarding the molecule.
  • Then Dushin is ran to calculate the reorganization energy spread amongst normal modes.

  • Csv files containing the relevant information is transferred to a local folder

A rough flow chart of the script made during its conception.

Database

The next part to develop a tool for systematic study is to make a database with the following requirements:

  • Self-generate using local files (Updated when files are updated)
  • Easily accessed and indexed (Cable to quickly return subsections relevant to eg. a specific type of molecules)
  • Ease of visualisation of data

Due to these considerations, I decided to use a multi index Pandas to construct the data frame as it can be easily generated from files, is very lightweight, supports conditional indexing and has inbuilt plotting capabilities as well as support for packages such as seaborn for more advanced visualisation.
